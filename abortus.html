The Journey

Candy, reconsidered.

We didn't set out to reinvent the wheel. We set out to make the wheel taste better.

01

The why

Most candy hasn't really changed in fifty years. Same wax. Same neon dyes. Same five-syllable preservatives nobody can pronounce. The packaging gets a refresh every decade or two, but underneath — same product.

We started RYDE because the standard had drifted. Coffee got better. Chocolate got better. Even gum got better. Candy stayed stuck in 1978.

"We wanted candy that didn't apologize for itself."

02

The build

How we got here.

2024

The kitchen experiments

A garage. A candy thermometer. Forty-seven failed batches of chewy citrus. One that finally worked. We knew we had something.

2025

The brand takes shape

We named it RYDE. We picked our colors — black, bone, a single line of gold. We refused to look like anything else on the shelf.

2026

The first drop

Slicks and Drifters launch worldwide. Two products. One mission. A bigger lineup already in the pipeline.

03

The rules

What we won't compromise on.

→ Real first

If the flavor on the label is strawberry, real strawberry is in the bag. No hiding behind "natural flavor".

→ Design forward

Packaging that earns counter space. We treat the bag like an object, not just a wrapper.

→ No shortcuts

Slower production. Smaller batches. Higher quality. We'd rather miss a launch than miss the bar.

→ Make it move

Candy is fun. We don't take ourselves too seriously — even when we take the craft seriously.
